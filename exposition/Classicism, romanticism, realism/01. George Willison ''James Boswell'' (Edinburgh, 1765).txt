James Boswell, 9th Laird of Auchinleck (1740–1795), was a Scottish biographer, diarist, and lawyer, born in Edinburgh. He is best known for his biography of his friend and older contemporary, the English writer Samuel Johnson, which is commonly said to be the greatest biography written in the English language. NL NL
The owl perched on a branch nearby signifies the artist's intent to portray Boswell as a man of learning and wisdom, despite (in this picture) his relative youth. Or it may suggest his delight in night-time activities – Boswell had many sexual adventures in Italy.
